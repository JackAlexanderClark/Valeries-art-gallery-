Valerie's English Scenes

Biography

I grew up in Cheshire, later educated at Cheadle County Grammar for Girls, Cheshire. Grew up in the 60s - (not swinging--- but in the Industrious Northern suburbs!) and my first work at 17 was in Textiles, art reproduction and Graphics before becoming a Tech/ information Officer in the Civil Service for many years.

Where I am from

Prior to that, ran a Country Inn with my husband, which nestled in the slopes of the Malvern Hills, and was constantly inspired there by the sheer beauty and musicality of the Shires. And Art was always there for me, for myself and my own creative efforts, but also with a burgeoning interest in Classical art, even around the hard-working career and family days.

Style

I am more or less self- taught, apart from my early Textile Design reproduction days when I attended a course at the Faculty of Technology Manchester for Graphics/Printing processes but during my Worcestershire years I did work with the Worcester/Severn tutor Watercolourist, Robin Holder. My Style is eclectic and sometimes arbitrary but always influenced by a Light source. I work in Oils, Acrylics and Watercolours when painting Plein Air. I like to sketch with charcoal and for quick compositions and portrait, use Pastels.

Influences

Today I can reflect on my Westerley sojourn down ------from Cheshire, and then being in the midst of the Three Jewel counties of Worcestershire, Herefordshire and Shropshire. More recently, where I live now, and sure to retire, ----- here in Dramatic Cornwall. I have been inspired by the incredulous beauty of all these Shires of Western England and they have influenced my sketchings and paintings and I hope Cornwall will inspire even more. My collection is essentially “the English Scene”.
